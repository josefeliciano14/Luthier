CNC

I was approached by a client to restore his Squier Stratocaster. The repairs would cost more than the guitar is worth but the guitar had sentimental value so it was worth it to him.

The first step was to use an iron to heat up the glue and remove the fretboard without damage.

The truss rod was replaced and the fretboard was then reglued.

The frets were removed and the fingerboard was sanded to ensure a level surface.

The fingerboard was refretted and a level/crown/polish was done on them to make them as playable as possible.
